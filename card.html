Siberian Tiger

Siberian Tigers, also known as Amur tigers, are the largest cats in the world.

  • Scientific Name: Panthera tigris altaica
  • Average Length: 11ft (3.3m)
  • Average Weight: 700lbs (320kg)
  • Habitat: North Korea, China, and Russia

Distinguished by their striped fur, no two tigers stripes are identical. The stripes can be thought of akin to fingerprints. The Siberian Tigers have fewer, paler stripes and also have manes. Manes, as well as thick fur, help keep them warm in the cold conditions.
